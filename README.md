# Chilean Marine Seismic Lines — Paper Database

A curated database of scientific papers about marine seismic surveys (reflection and refraction) along the Chilean coast and offshore subduction zone, spanning from northern Chile (~18°S) to the southernmost tip (~57°S). Each paper entry includes full acquisition metadata, dataset access links, and processing workflow details extracted from the original publications.

## Key Observations

1. **Erosion-to-accretion transition**: The Chilean margin transitions from tectonic erosion (north, ~17°–33°S) to accretionary subduction (south, ~33°–57°S). CINCA95 (north) and SPOC/TIPTEQ (south) seismic campaigns directly image this contrast.

2. **Megathrust reflectivity and earthquake rupture**: PICTURES (MGL1610) data at 19°–21°S show that high plate-boundary reflectivity updip of the 2014 Iquique rupture indicates excess fluid pressure and aseismic behavior, while the rupture zone shows absent reflectivity (Ma et al. 2022, Nature Communications).

3. **Basal accretion at south-central Chile**: CEVICHE (MGL1701) long-offset data (Bangs et al. 2020, Arnulf et al. 2022) image underplated oceanic material beneath the wedge at 30°–44°S, with implications for the seismogenic zone and Maule/Valdivia earthquake rupture patterns.

4. **Trench sediment control**: The abundance of trench sediment south of ~33°S is a primary control on accretionary vs. erosive subduction mode. Sediment thickness increases southward from near-zero at Antofagasta to >2.5 km at the Arauco transect (Olsen et al. 2020).

5. **Oceanic plate hydration**: Multiple OBS wide-angle studies at the outer rise (Contreras-Reyes et al. 2007 at ~43°S, Moscoso & Grevemeyer 2015 at 34°–35°S) show reduced uppermost mantle velocities (Vp 7.4–7.8 km/s) indicating significant serpentinization through bending-related normal faults.

6. **Chile Triple Junction**: A distinctive thermal and structural domain at ~46°S where the actively spreading Chile Ridge enters the subduction zone. Conrad RC2901 MCS data (Bangs & Cande 1997; Brown et al. 1996) and GEOMAR OBS data (Maksymowicz et al. 2012) constrain the ridge-trench collision structure.

7. **Juan Fernández Ridge flat slab**: The subducting Juan Fernández Ridge creates a flat slab segment at ~29°–33°S (CONDOR experiment, von Huene et al. 1997; Laursen et al. 2002), fundamentally altering the margin structure and arc volcanism in this region.

8. **Gas hydrate system**: A continuous gas hydrate system imaged by BSR in MCS data extends from ~36°S to ~57°S, with heat flow controlled by sediment supply, the Chile Ridge thermal anomaly, and proximity to the Juan Fernández Ridge. Vargas-Cordero et al. (2010–2019) provide systematic coverage.

## Contributing

1. Copy `papers/schema.json` as a template
2. Fill in all required fields: `id`, `title`, `authors`, `year`, `journal`, `doi`, `url`, `location`, `acquisition`, `data`, and `processing`
3. Name the file using the convention: `lastname_year_keyword.json`
4. Place it in the `papers/` directory
5. Run the Streamlit app to verify the entry renders correctly:

```bash
streamlit run app.py
```
